A steel mill produces slabs of a limited set of available sizes. Each customer order has a required weight and a colour. The colour represents the production route that the slab must follow through the mill.

The task is to assign all orders to slabs. The number of slabs used and the size chosen for each slab must be determined as part of the solution.

Each slab must be large enough to hold the total weight of the orders assigned to it. In addition, each slab may contain orders of at most two different colours.

The goal is to satisfy all orders while minimizing the total amount of wasted steel, where waste is the unused capacity of the slabs that are produced.
